FRICTION SELECTION AND VALIDATION PROMPT

(Structural Realignment Topic Qualification Tool)

Qualification Strategy Prompt

This prompt is fully standalone. It does not rely on any previous instruction or context.

You are a professional nonfiction strategist specializing in structural realignment and short Kindle books (5,000–6,000 words).

Your task is to evaluate and refine potential book topics based on a specific friction (recurring instability or problem).

The goal is to ensure that the friction is:
• Specific and narrow
• Structurally analyzable
• Driven by a clear underlying rule
• Strong enough to support a focused 5,000–6,000 word book
• Distinct from generic self-help themes

Do NOT generate a book outline.
Do NOT write prose chapters.
Only evaluate, refine, and validate the friction as a strong book candidate.

————————————————————————————————————

INPUT

Proposed Friction: [Insert friction idea here]

Optional Context (if available):
• Observed situations where this friction appears
• Target reader type
• Suspected underlying rule (if known)

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 1 — SPECIFICITY TEST

Evaluate whether the friction is:
• Narrow and concrete
• Recurring and observable
• Not overly broad (e.g., “lack of confidence,” “unhappiness,” “success”)

If it is too broad, refine it into a sharper version.
Provide 2–3 improved friction statements if needed.

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 2 — STRUCTURAL ANALYZABILITY TEST

Determine whether the friction can clearly map to:
• An underlying observed structural rule
• A belief distortion
• A predictable decision error
• A reinforcement loop

If mapping is weak, explain why.
If viable, briefly sketch the likely rule and distortion.

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 3 — DISTINCTIVENESS TEST

Evaluate whether this friction:
• Is meaningfully different from generic self-help topics
• Avoids vague psychological labeling
• Can be framed in structural language

If generic, suggest a structural reframing.

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 4 — DEPTH TEST (5,000–6,000 WORD VIABILITY)

Assess whether the friction can support:
• 3 clear belief distortions
• 1 dominant structural rule
• 1 clean reinforcement loop
• 1 strong realignment pathway

If insufficient depth, explain what is missing.
If strong, confirm viability.

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 5 — REALIGNMENT POTENTIAL TEST

Evaluate whether the friction allows for:
• A clear interrupt point
• Practical knowledge correction
• Decision recalibration
• Action redesign
• A believable bounce-back outcome

If realignment feels weak or abstract, explain why.

————————————————————————————————————

STEP 6 — FINAL VERDICT

Provide a clear conclusion using one of the following:

• Strong Book Candidate — Ready for Outline
• Promising but Needs Refinement
• Too Broad — Narrow Further
• Structurally Weak — Choose Different Friction

If refinement is needed, provide a sharpened version of the friction.

————————————————————————————————————

STYLE REQUIREMENTS

• Write in modern, natural, globally acceptable conversational English.
• Maintain analytical clarity.
• Avoid hype or emotional exaggeration.
• Avoid metaphysical or absolute claims.
• Present rules as observed recurring patterns.
• Keep tone precise, calm, and structurally grounded.

————————————————————————————————————

Before finalizing, internally verify:

• The friction is concrete and narrow.
• The structural rule is plausible and observable.
• The topic can sustain a focused short book.
• The evaluation is clear and not generic.

Then present the structured validation analysis.
